    Lars Hinrichs at the WSJ TechCafe in Berlin.

    German entrepreneur Lars Hinrichs has pulled the plug on his HackFwd accelerator. But rather than retreat in silence, Mr. Hinrichs, who founded Xing AG — a sort of German LinkedIn Corp. — in 2003, ¬†has put up a great blog post that provides a feast of links to some of the lectures and talks the accelerator has hosted.

    Mr. Hinrichs invested about “8 million” (he doesn’t say what currency, but probably either dollars or euros) of his own money that came from the sale of his stake in Xing in 2009 to the German media group Hubert Burda Media. He set up the accelerator three years ago to help foster coders to build companies.

    But, he said times had changed, and: “There are literally thousands of accelerators; so many that I often wonder if there are more accelerators than angels and more angels than there are entrepreneurs.”

    But what makes Mr. Hinrichs’s post a must-read is the list of 26 videos from the likes of Amazon Inc. CTO Werner Vogels, Ideo’s Tom Hulme, and Mangrove Capital Partners’ Michael Jackson that is an accelerator program in and of itself and one that any wannabe entrepreneur would do well to dedicate the few hours it will take them to soak in all that advice.

    According to Mr. Hinrichs, the five things HackFwd got right were:

    1. Geeks Can Be CEOs
    2. European Geeks Are As Talented As American Ones
    3. Mentorship Is Worth More Than Money
    4. The Need For In-Person Inspiration
    5. The Importance Of Intellectual Generosity

    And the five things they got wrong:

    1. We Didn‚Äôt Cut Early Enough
    2. It Was Difficult To Nurture And Scale
    3. We Underestimated The Bureaucracy
    4. We Assumed Faster Exit Times
    5. It Can Be Hard To Go Deep And Wide

    He was one of the group of leading European entrepreneurs that recently presented a startup manifesto to Neelie Kroes, European commissioner for the digital agenda, setting out what they believe to be the steps necessary for Europe’s digital economy to thrive.
