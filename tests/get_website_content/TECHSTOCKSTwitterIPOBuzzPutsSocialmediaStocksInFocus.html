TECH STOCKS: Twitter IPO Buzz Puts Social-media Stocks In Focus

09/13/2013 | 04:22pm US/Eastern
Recommend:
0

By Rex Crum, MarketWatch

SAN FRANCISCO (MarketWatch) -- Talk about Twitter filing to go public was the buzz of Wall Street Friday, and social-media companies such as Facebook Inc. and other Internet stocks got some attention from investors.

Twitter made the announcement via a tweet on the company's official Twitter account late Thursday. The company said little about its plans other than it "confidentially submitted" its S-1 form to the Securities and Exchange Commission as part of its plan to go public.

One of Twitter's top investors, GSV Capital (NEXT), last month reportedly valued Twitter at $10.5 billion.

Elsewhere in the social-media and Internet sector, Facebook Inc. (>> Facebook Inc) was off 1% to close at $44.31, LinkedIn Corp. (>> LinkedIn Corp) edged down by a fraction to close at $249.59, Yelp Inc. (>> Yelp Inc) shed 1.4% to close at $62.91and Yahoo Inc. (>> Yahoo! Inc.) gave up 1.3% to close at $29.26.

Among other leading tech stocks, Apple Inc. (>> Apple Inc.) was down 1.7% to close at $464.90, and losses also came from Amazon.com Inc. (>> Amazon.com, Inc.), Groupon Inc. (>> Groupon Inc) and Priceline.com Inc. (>> Priceline.com Inc).

One notable gainer was Intel Corp. (>> Intel Corporation), with the world's largest semiconductor company's stock rising 3.6% to close at $23.44.

Jefferies analyst Mark Lipacis raised his rating on Intel to buy from hold, and also lifted his price target on the stock to $30 from $27. In a research note, Lipacis cited Intel's efforts in the mobile and lowest-cost tablet markets among the reasons for his upgrade.

"[Intel] is finally focusing its manufacturing leadership to make MPUs [mobile processing chips] that are lower power, higher performance and cheaper than competitive solutions," Lipacis said. "This new focus should translate to share gains in sub-$500 tablets, 2-in-1 PCs and handsets."

The Nasdaq Composite Index (RIXF) rose 0.2% to close at 3,722.18, ending the week with a 1.7% gain.

The Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) added 0.5%.

Stocks mentioned in the article : Apple Inc., Yelp Inc, Priceline.com Inc, LinkedIn Corp, Intel Corporation, Groupon Inc, Facebook Inc, Amazon.com, Inc., Yahoo! Inc.
