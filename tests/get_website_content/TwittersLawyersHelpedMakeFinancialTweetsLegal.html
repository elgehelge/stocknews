Twitter’s Lawyers Helped Make Financial Tweets Legal

Law firm Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati is serving as Twitter Inc.’s legal counsel on its nascent initial public offering, according to people familiar with the matter.

What’s most notable about that is that the Palo Alto-based global law firm was also some of the legal muscle behind the Securities and Exchange Commission’s move earlier this year to bless Twitter as an outlet for companies to announce material business news.

That came about when Netflix Inc. chief executive Reed Hastings posted viewing stats for his streaming video service to his Facebook page in July 2012. A few months later, the SEC sent him and Netlix a “Wells Notice” that the agency could bring action for violating rules about fair disclosure of information to investors.

But Netflix and Mr. Hastings, with Wilson Sonsini as their counsel, according to the firm’s website, ended up getting the SEC to make an about-face. In April, the agency announced that social media is an acceptable way to disclose information to investors, as long as the company says that social media is a place they may make announcements. (Activist investor Carl Icahn did this recently.)

In addition, the firm’s ties with Twitter run deep. Both Twitter’s new general counsel, Vijaya Gadde, and its previous legal head, Alexander Macgillivray, have worked at Wilson Sonsini.

As far as Twitter’s secret JOBS Act IPO filing goes, the company could be making use of the expertise of Wilson Sonsini  partner Steve Bochner, one of two lawyers who served on the task force that helped formulate a lot of the ideas behind the law.

Like bankers, lawyers seek notable deal assignments, to generate fees, boost their brand, and win bragging rights. Wilson Sonsini advised LinkedIn Corp. on its IPO in 2011, but missed out on Facebook Inc. last year, which worked with rival Fenwick & West LLP.

Along with Cooley LLP, the three firms have historically dominated high-profile Silicon Valley IPO work. So far this year, each of the firms has represented 3 tech company IPOs in the U.S., according to figures from Dealogic, the most of any firms.

A spokeswoman for Wilson Sonsini did not respond to requests for comment.
