Market Wrap-Up for Sept. 13 (RAI, INTC, AMGN, COV, more)

By Michael Flannelly|September 13th, 2013
  • Tweet
RSS

We saw some choppy early this morning, with the major indices mixed as we headed towards the afternoon. The release of disappointing Consumer Confidence and Retail Sales data brought about a bit of tepid sentiment from investors and traders. However, by the close, stocks were still able to edge out some gains.

Stocks on the Rise

Among the stocks in positive territory today were: Reynolds America (RAI), after it announced that it would be offering $1.1 billion in bonds; Amgen (AMGN), after its VP of Investor Relations hinted at meaningful dividend increases over the next two years; Dunkin Brands (DNKN), extending yesterday’s gains following the announcement that it would be entering the U.K. market; and Philip Morris International (PM), adding to its gains following yesterday’s dividend increase announcement.

Furthermore, due to Wall Street analysts’ upgrades, shares of Safeway (SWY), Aetna (AET), Dominion Resources (D), SanDisk (SNDK), Maximus (MMS), Kroger (KR), and Intel (INTC) also rallied today.

Stocks on the Decline

In the red today were shares of Covidien (COV), as investors have not reacted well to the company’s 2014 growth strategies.

Also, shares of Apple (AAPL) and Noble Corp. (NE) closed in negative territory following Wall Street analysts’ downgrades.

Be sure to check the Dividend Daily for all the latest earnings reports, analyst moves, and much more.

Twitter’s IPO: Don’t Get Caught Up in the Excitement

The time has finally come: Twitter announced after the close yesterday that it has filed for its initial public offering. In what is the most highly anticipated IPO since Facebook (FB), investors are expected to line up to get a piece of the micro-blogging site. Last night and this morning I’ve heard segments on every program from “Fast Money” to “Good Morning America” giving you, the average investor, tips on how to cash in on Twitter’s IPO. But should you listen to these reports? Should you get caught up in the Twitter IPO euphoria? I say no.

Though it can be tempting to jump on the bandwagon and grab a stock like Twitter when it finally trades publicly, there are many risks involved that are undesirable for long-term investors. Especially with Twitter, where the details around its financials, growth potential, and long-term outlook–all very important to dividend/long-term investors–remain unclear, jumping in on the IPO hoopla could just end up being a risky trade, not an informed investment.

In general, we advise that dividend investors avoid joining in on the frenzy for these types of high-profile IPOs of tech and growth stocks. While they have the potential for a lot of upside gains (see: LinkedIn), there comes a lot of risks with that potential reward. Just look at Facebook, which just crossed its initial offering price of $38 weeks ago, 15 months after its IPO. For those investors who bought up FB stock in those initial trading sessions after its IPO, it look them over a year to see gains. Yes, there is always the possibility that Twitter ends up being a Wall Street superstar, but for dividend investors who want stable, long-term returns, the downside risks outweigh this potential upside.

When it comes to saving, investing, and building wealth, we don’t want to jump into whatever asset is the hot topic on the news or at cocktail parties. This type of blind, emotional, and uninformed investing tends to be a losing proposition. For every IPO success story, there is a Zynga (ZNGA) or another IPO dud. So, dividend investors should make sure to stick to well established, dividend paying stocks on their way towards building wealth. It may not be the sexiest route to see long-term gains, but it still remains the most effective strategy.

Looking Toward Next Week

Next week we get quarterly earnings from Cintas Corp (CTAS), Cracker Barrel (CBRL), Darden Restaurants (DRI), Oracle (ORCL), FactSet (FDS), FedEx (FDX), General Mills (GIS), and ConAgra Foods (CAG). Also, the Federal Reserve holds its September FOMC meeting on Tuesday and Wednesday, after which it will announce the future of its monetary policies and stimulus. So, expect a bit of volatility in the markets stemming from the meeting, its statement, and Fed Chairman Ben Bernanke’s subsequent press conference.
